About Me

Bridging Research, Education & Public Health Practice

I work at the intersection of academia, research, and frontline healthcare — committed to reducing health inequalities and improving emergency care access for underserved communities.

As a Fellow of the Royal Society for Public Health (FRSPH), I bring a rigorous evidence-based lens to every project. My current work as Principal Investigator at the Yorkshire Ambulance Service (YAS) Research Institute focuses on understanding how deprivation affects access to emergency care in Yorkshire — backed by a £9,850 pump-priming grant. Alongside my research role, I lecture in Health and Care Management at Arden University, shaping the next generation of healthcare professionals. I am also an NHS Pathways Trainer, conducting compliance audits and competency assessments for remote care staff.
